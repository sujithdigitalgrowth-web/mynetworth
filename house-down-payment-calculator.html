📖 How Much Down Payment for a House in India?

When you buy a home in India, the bank doesn't fund the entire amount. You need to pay a portion upfront — that's the down payment. Here's what you should know:

Minimum: 10–15% of property value (for home loans up to ₹30L)

Recommended: 20% of property value — lower EMI, better interest rates, less total interest paid

Additional costs: Stamp duty (5–7%), registration (1–2%), broker fee, interior work — budget an extra 10–12% beyond the down payment

For example, for a ₹80 lakh flat with 20% down payment: you need ₹16 lakh as down payment + approximately ₹5.6 lakh for stamp duty and registration = around ₹21.6 lakh total upfront.

💡 Tips for Saving Your Down Payment

🎯

Set a Deadline

Pick a date — "I want to buy a home by Dec 2028." Work backwards to find monthly savings needed.

📈

Invest While Saving

If buying in 3+ years, put savings in a balanced hybrid fund. For 1–2 years, use a debt fund or RD.

💳

Clear Debts First

Banks check your existing EMIs. Lower debt = higher loan eligibility = easier approval.

🏦

Improve CIBIL Score

750+ score gets you the best interest rates. Pay credit cards on time and keep utilization under 30%.

❓ FAQ

Can I buy a house with 0% down payment?
Very unlikely in India. Banks require at least 10% down payment for most home loans. Some government schemes offer subsidies, but you'll still need cash upfront for stamp duty and registration.
Should I pay a larger down payment?
If you can afford it, yes. A larger down payment means smaller loan, lower EMI, less interest paid over the loan tenure, and often a better interest rate from the bank.
What other costs should I budget for?
Beyond down payment: stamp duty (5–7%), registration (1–2%), GST on under-construction property (5%), broker commission (1–2%), home loan processing fee (0.5–1%), interior/renovation costs. Budget an extra 10–15% of property value.
