📖 What is Net Worth?

Net worth is the simplest way to measure your financial health. It's calculated as:

Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities

Assets = Savings accounts + Fixed deposits + Mutual funds + Stocks + Property + Gold + PPF/EPF + NPS + anything else you own

Liabilities = Home loan + Car loan + Personal loan + Gold loan + Credit card debt + anything else you owe

If your assets are more than your liabilities, your net worth is positive — you own more than you owe. If it's negative, don't panic. Most people start with negative net worth (think student loans, home loans). What matters is the direction — is it going up over time?

💡 Why Does Net Worth Matter?

Your salary tells you how much you earn. Your net worth tells you how much you've kept. Here's why tracking it matters:

  • It gives you one honest number instead of scattered bank balances
  • It shows whether you're actually making financial progress year over year
  • It helps you understand if your assets are growing faster than your debts
  • It shifts your mindset from "how much do I earn" to "how much do I own"
  • It's the number that actually determines if you can retire, buy a house, or be financially free

Net Worth Benchmarks for India

Age 25–30: Net worth = 1–3x annual income (you're building the base)
Age 30–35: Net worth = 3–5x annual income (compounding kicks in)
Age 35–45: Net worth = 5–10x annual income (peak earning years)
Age 45+: Net worth = 10x+ annual income (retirement prep)

These are rough guidelines. The most important thing is that your net worth is going up every year.

🚀 How to Improve Your Net Worth

There are only two levers: grow your assets or reduce your liabilities. Here are practical ways:

💰

Build an Emergency Fund

Keep 6 months of expenses in a savings account or liquid fund. This stops you from borrowing during emergencies.

🔥

Pay Off High-Interest Debt

Credit cards (36%), personal loans (14–18%) destroy wealth. Pay these off first before investing.

📈

Start a Monthly SIP

Even ₹5,000/month in an index fund grows to ₹50+ lakhs in 20 years at 12% returns.

🏠

Own Appreciating Assets

Stocks, mutual funds, and property appreciate. Cars and electronics depreciate. Choose wisely.

📊

Track Monthly

What gets measured gets managed. Check your net worth every month — it becomes a habit.

🛡️

Get Insurance

Term life + health insurance protects your family's net worth from unexpected events.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What should I include in assets?
Include everything you own that has monetary value: bank savings, fixed deposits, mutual funds, stocks, EPF/PPF balance, NPS, real estate (market value), gold/jewellery, vehicles, and any other investments.
What counts as liabilities?
Any money you owe: home loan outstanding, car loan, personal loan, gold loan, education loan, credit card dues, EMIs on purchases, and any borrowed money from family or friends.
Is negative net worth bad?
Not necessarily. If you recently took a home loan, your net worth will likely be negative. That's normal. What matters is the trend — your net worth should increase every year. Focus on paying down debt and building assets.
How often should I calculate my net worth?
Once a month is ideal. It takes just 5 minutes and helps you see real progress. Our dashboard makes it automatic — just update your asset values and it tracks everything.
What's a good net worth for my age in India?
A common benchmark is your net worth should be at least your annual income multiplied by your age divided by 10. For example, if you're 30 and earn ₹12L/year, target ₹36L. But don't compare — focus on growing yours consistently.
