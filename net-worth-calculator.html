Calculate your net worth online in seconds — free, simple, and no signup needed. Your net worth is the single most honest number about your financial health: everything you own minus everything you owe.

Net worth meaning: Net worth is the difference between your total assets (what you own) and your total liabilities (what you owe). A positive net worth means you own more than you owe. A negative net worth means your debts currently exceed your assets — which is normal at the start of a financial journey.

Unlike your salary, which shows what you earn, net worth shows what you've actually built. Two people with the same income can have completely different net worths based on their savings, spending, and debt habits. Think of it like a company's balance sheet — assets on one side, liabilities on the other, net worth is what's left over. The higher it is, the more financial security and freedom you have.

Net worth is not the same as income. A government employee earning ₹8 LPA who saves diligently for 10 years can have a higher net worth than a startup employee earning ₹30 LPA who spends everything. Income is what flows in. Net worth is what stays.

🧾 How to Calculate Net Worth in India — Step by Step

Calculating your net worth in India takes three steps: list your assets, list your liabilities, and subtract. Here's exactly what to include:

  1. List all your Assets (what you own)

    Savings accounts, salary account balance, fixed deposits (FDs), recurring deposits, liquid mutual funds, equity mutual funds (current NAV value), stocks in demat (current market value), PPF balance, EPF balance, NPS corpus, Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana, sovereign gold bonds, physical gold at today's market rate, real estate (current market value, not purchase price), vehicle resale value, and any other investments.

  2. List all your Liabilities (what you owe)

    Home loan outstanding principal, car loan balance, personal loan balance, gold loan, education loan, credit card total outstanding (not just minimum due), EMI purchases (phone, laptop, appliances), and any money borrowed from family or friends you intend to repay.

  3. Calculate: Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities

    Use the calculator above. Positive means you own more than you owe — great! Negative means your debts currently exceed your assets — this is common early on (home loans, education loans) and improves over time as you pay down debt and build assets.

  4. Track it every month

    One calculation is informative. Monthly tracking is transformative. Set a reminder on the 1st of every month to update your values and recalculate. The trend — not the single number — is what builds financial discipline.

Salary tells you what you earn. Net worth tells you what you've built. The difference is enormous — and understanding it changes how you make financial decisions.

📈

Tracks Real Financial Progress

Your salary can stay flat while your net worth grows (through investing), or your salary can rise while net worth stays stagnant (through lifestyle inflation). Net worth shows the truth.

🏦

Is the Key to Financial Freedom

🚀 How to Improve Your Net Worth in India

There are only two levers to improve net worth: grow your assets or reduce your liabilities. The most powerful approach is doing both simultaneously. Here's how:

💸

Increase Your Savings Rate

Save at least 20–30% of take-home income. Every extra rupee saved today is a seed for compounding. The savings rate matters more than investment returns in the early years.

🔥

Eliminate High-Interest Debt First

Credit cards (24–42% p.a.) and personal loans (12–18% p.a.) are wealth destroyers. Pay these off completely before investing. Clearing ₹1L in 36% credit card debt is a guaranteed 36% return.

📈

Start or Increase Monthly SIPs

Investing ₹10,000/month in a Nifty 50 index fund at 12% CAGR grows to over ₹1 crore in 20 years. The key is starting early and staying consistent — don't pause SIPs in market dips.

🏠

Own Appreciating Assets

Stocks, mutual funds, gold, and well-located real estate appreciate over time. Cars, electronics, and gadgets depreciate to near zero. Shift your asset mix toward appreciating assets.

🛡️

Get Adequate Insurance

A single uninsured medical emergency can destroy years of wealth building. Term life + health insurance costs very little relative to the risk they protect against. Insure first, invest second.

📅

Track Monthly Without Fail

Set a recurring reminder on the 1st of every month. Update asset values and recalculate. People who track their net worth regularly build wealth 2–3× faster than those who don't — measurement creates motivation.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is net worth?
Net worth is the total value of everything you own (assets) minus everything you owe (liabilities). It is the most comprehensive and honest single measure of your financial health — more meaningful than salary, bank balance, or income alone. A positive net worth means your assets exceed your debts; a negative net worth means your debts currently exceed your assets.
How to calculate net worth in India?
To calculate net worth in India: (1) Add up all assets — savings accounts, FDs, mutual funds, stocks, EPF/PPF, NPS, gold, real estate market value, vehicle resale value; (2) Add up all liabilities — home loan outstanding, car loan, personal loan, credit card dues, any other debts; (3) Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities. Use today's market values for assets, not original purchase prices.
What is a good net worth in India?
A commonly used benchmark: net worth should be approximately 1–3× annual income by age 30, 3–6× by age 35, and 6–10× by age 45. For retirement readiness, target 25–30× your annual expenses. However, the most important thing is not the absolute number but consistent upward growth — your net worth should increase meaningfully every year.
Does salary affect net worth?
Salary provides the raw material to build net worth, but it does not determine it. What matters is how much of your income you save and invest. Two people earning the same salary can have vastly different net worths — entirely due to spending habits, debt decisions, and investment consistency. Net worth is what you keep, not what you earn.
How often should I calculate my net worth?
Once a month is ideal. On the 1st of each month, update your asset values (check mutual fund NAVs, stock portfolio, and loan outstanding) and recalculate your net worth. It takes 5–10 minutes and provides the monthly financial check-in that most wealth builders swear by.
Should I include my house in net worth?
Yes — include your home's current market value (not purchase price) in assets, and your outstanding home loan in liabilities. The difference is your home equity, which counts toward net worth. Remember that your primary residence is an illiquid asset, so also track your liquid net worth (financial assets minus debts) separately for planning purposes.
Is negative net worth bad?
Not necessarily — it depends on why it's negative. If you recently took a home loan for a property that will appreciate, your net worth will be temporarily negative but improving over time. However, if it's negative due to credit card debt or personal loans, those are urgent problems to fix. The key is whether your net worth is improving each month.
