How Jeff Bezos Built His Fortune

Bezos founded Amazon in 1994 from a Seattle garage as an online bookstore. The 1997 IPO was modest by today's standards, but the trajectory thereafter was unmatched. Amazon Web Services (AWS) launched in 2006, and within 15 years became the dominant cloud-computing platform globally and the single largest contributor to Amazon's profits. Bezos also founded Blue Origin in 2000, originally focused on suborbital tourism but increasingly oriented toward orbital launches with the New Glenn rocket programme.

The 2019 divorce from MacKenzie Scott reduced his Amazon stake (she retained 4 percent of shares she received). He stepped down as Amazon CEO in July 2021 to focus on Blue Origin, the Bezos Earth Fund (with USD 10 billion climate commitments), and other investments. The 2025 Lauren Sanchez wedding in Venice (estimated cost USD 46–56 million) marked the most public personal event of his post-CEO chapter. Blue Origin's New Glenn first successful orbital launch in January 2025 marked the operational milestone.

Income Sources in May 2026

Real Estate and Property Holdings

Indian Creek Island, Miami (the 'Billionaire Bunker'): three neighbouring properties acquired 2023–2024 for USD 237 million-plus combined; primary residence. Beverly Hills Warner Estate: bought in 2020 from David Geffen for USD 165 million (record-setting at the time); a 9-acre Georgian estate originally built by Jack Warner. Adjacent Beverly Hills estate purchased to expand the compound. Additional holdings in Maui (Hawaii), New York City, and Washington DC.

Yacht, Jets, and Notable Assets

Koru superyacht: approximately USD 500 million, three-masted schooner, one of the world's largest sailing yachts. The Abeona support vessel houses helicopters and additional crew. Gulfstream G650ER private jet fleet handles global travel. The Koru is a defining personal asset, as visible globally as Larry Ellison's various yachts and Roman Abramovich's pre-sanction Eclipse.
