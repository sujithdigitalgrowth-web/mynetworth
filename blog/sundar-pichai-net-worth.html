How Sundar Pichai Got from Madurai to $1.6 Billion

Pichai Sundararajan was born on 10 June 1972 in Madurai, Tamil Nadu. He grew up in Ashok Nagar, Chennai, where his father Regunatha Pichai was a senior electrical engineer at GEC (General Electric Company of UK) — a manufacturing and R&D role that meant the family was educated and technically literate but not wealthy. The family lived in a modest two-room apartment on Jubilee Road in Ashok Nagar. There was no car in the household; Sundar grew up travelling by two-wheeler and public transport. He has spoken in interviews about having a good upbringing despite material limitations, and about his father's deep investment in education.

Sundar studied at Jawahar Vidyalaya in Chennai for his early schooling and then at Vana Vani school for secondary education. He gained admission to the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, graduating with a bachelor's degree in metallurgical engineering with a silver medal — the second-highest academic distinction. He then moved to the United States on a scholarship at Stanford University, where he completed a master's degree in Materials Science and Engineering. He followed this with an MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, where he was named both a Siebel Scholar and a Palmer Scholar.

Before Google, Pichai worked in engineering and product management at Applied Materials, and then as a management consultant at McKinsey & Company. He joined Google in April 2004 as a product manager, reporting into the search product team. His early impact at Google was outsized — he championed the development of Google Chrome, the web browser that launched in 2008 and became the world's most used browser. He led the development of the Android OS product strategy, Google Drive, and the ChromeOS platform. In 2013, he turned down a CEO offer from Twitter that would have made him one of the wealthiest technology executives overnight.

In August 2015, Larry Page named Pichai CEO of Google as part of the restructuring that created the Alphabet holding company. In December 2019, Pichai was further named CEO of Alphabet as well when Larry Page and Sergey Brin stepped back from their executive roles. Under Pichai's leadership, Alphabet has navigated multiple antitrust actions in the US and EU, the rise of TikTok and short-form video content, and most recently the AI-era disruption from ChatGPT — responding with the launch of Gemini, the successor to Google's Bard AI assistant.

Pichai's wealth crossed approximately $1.1 billion in July 2025 during the AI-era Alphabet rally. By February 2026, Bloomberg estimated his net worth at approximately $1.6 billion per Wikipedia's data — a significant jump from the approximately $1.4 billion estimated by Forbes in late 2024.

Sundar Pichai: Personal Life and Assets

Sundar Pichai is married to Anjali Pichai (née Haryani), whom he met as a classmate at IIT Kharagpur. They married in 1997. The couple have two children — a son named Kiran and a daughter named Kavya. The family lives in Los Altos Hills, California, in a house valued at approximately $40 million. He became a US citizen in 2017.

Pichai's car collection is understated relative to his wealth — he primarily drives an Audi Q7. He is known in Silicon Valley for a relatively modest and low-profile personal lifestyle for someone of his wealth level. He is an avid football (soccer) fan — he supported the Indian national team as a child — and has cited cricket as another passion. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 2022 — one of the highest civilian honours in India — alongside Satya Nadella.
