How Zepto Got to $7 Billion

The Origin Story

  • 2020 pandemic — Aadit & Kaivalya, both 19, stuck at home in Mumbai; groceries took 2–3 days
  • Started KiranaKart through Y Combinator; partnered with local stores for 45-min delivery
  • 2021: Rebranded to Zepto, built own dark stores — the breakthrough move
  • Contrary Capital offered to invest if they dropped out of Stanford — they did
  • Reached $500M valuation within 5 months; unicorn by end of year 2
  • October 2025: $7B valuation in CalPERS-led round

Aadit Palicha & Kaivalya Vohra: The Founders

Aadit Palicha (born October 15, 2001, Mumbai) scored a perfect 45/45 on the IB Diploma at GEMS Modern Academy, Dubai. He was admitted to Stanford's CS programme but dropped out during the pandemic. His net worth as of 2026 is approximately ₹5,380 crore ($650 million), overwhelmingly tied up in his Zepto equity.

Kaivalya Vohra, his childhood friend and CTO, has a comparable wealth profile. The two previously built GoPool — a carpooling app for Dubai school parents — at age 17, which received a grant from Emirates NBD bank. Both are now 24 and among India's youngest wealth creators.

The Business Model: How Dark Stores Work

Zepto operates 250+ small dark stores (4,000–5,000 sq ft) located within 1–2 km of dense customer clusters. Each store is stocked for the most-ordered items in its neighbourhood. AI-driven demand forecasting and pickers' workflow optimisation plus motorcycle delivery riders enabling the 10-minute promise. Take rates come from commissions, advertising, and adjacencies like Zepto Cafe and Zepto Nova.

Upcoming IPO

Zepto has filed its DRHP confidentially with SEBI for an IPO targeting ₹10,000 crore ($1.22 billion). With ~$900 million in net cash on the balance sheet, the company is well-funded heading into the listing. The key question for investors: can Zepto sustain growth without widening losses, especially as Blinkit reaches contribution profitability?
