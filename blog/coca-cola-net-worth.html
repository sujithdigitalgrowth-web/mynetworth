Origin Story

  • May 8, 1886: Pharmacist John Pemberton creates syrup at his Atlanta laboratory
  • First sold at Jacob's Pharmacy soda fountain — 9 glasses/day at 5 cents each
  • Bookkeeper Frank Robinson named it "Coca-Cola" and designed the Spencerian script logo
  • 1888: Asa Candler buys the formula from Pemberton for $2,300
  • 1892: The Coca-Cola Company incorporated in Atlanta by Candler
  • 1919: Sold to Ernest Woodruff and a consortium for $25 million
  • 1960–present: Global expansion to 200+ countries

The Portfolio: Far Beyond Coke

Coca-Cola owns or licenses 200+ brands globally. Key beverages: Sprite, Fanta, Diet Coke, Coke Zero Sugar, Minute Maid (juice), Dasani (water), Smartwater, Powerade (sports), Topo Chico (sparkling). In 2018, Coca-Cola paid $5.1 billion for Costa Coffee, entering the hot beverages segment.

In India, Coca-Cola re-entered in 1993 after a 16-year absence (having left in 1977 after the government demanded the secret formula). Today it owns local icons including Thums Up, Limca and Maaza — making India one of its most important emerging markets.

Buffett's 40-Year Hold

Warren Buffett began buying Coca-Cola in 1988 and never sold. Berkshire Hathaway owns approximately 9% of Coca-Cola today, worth roughly $24 billion. Buffett has described Coke as the ideal consumer brand — global, moated, pricing-power-driven, capital-light. He reportedly drinks 5 Cokes per day himself.
