158 Years of Firsts

1868: Jamsetji Tata founds the group in Bombay as a trading company.

1903: Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, Mumbai — India's first luxury hotel, reportedly built after Jamsetji was denied entry to a colonial hotel due to his ethnicity.

1907: Tata Steel (TISCO) — Asia's first integrated steel plant at Jamshedpur (now Jharkhand).

1909: Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru — co-founded with a ₹30 lakh endowment from Jamsetji.

2000: Tata Tea acquires Tetley (UK) for £271M — one of India's first major international acquisitions.

2004: TCS lists on BSE/NSE. Becomes India's most valuable IT company.

2007: Tata Steel acquires Corus (Anglo-Dutch) for £6.7B — then the largest Indian acquisition ever.

2008: Jaguar Land Rover acquired from Ford for $2.3B. JLR later becomes one of Tata Motors' most valuable assets.

2022–present: Tata Electronics manufactures Apple iPhones in India. Air India reacquired from government. Trent/Zudio becomes India's fastest-growing fashion retailer.

The Tata Ownership Structure

Who Really Owns Tata Group?

  • Tata Sons: Unlisted principal holding company that controls all major Tata companies
  • ~66% owned by philanthropic trusts: Sir Dorabji Tata Trust and Sir Ratan Tata Trust
  • These trusts fund hospitals, universities, and rural development — the income from 66% of Tata Sons goes to charity
  • N Chandrasekaran: Chairman Tata Sons since Feb 2017; former CEO of TCS
  • Tata family retains a minority stake; the group is philosophically owned by India's public interest
  • Spark Capital values Tata Sons at ₹7.8 lakh crore — unlisted but estimated
