The Aladdin Platform

Aladdin (Asset, Liability, Debt and Derivative Investment Network) is BlackRock's risk management software. It processes 250+ million data inputs daily and monitors $21.6 trillion in assets — including those managed by competitor firms that license Aladdin. This B2B software business provides BlackRock with sticky recurring revenue that is independent of market performance.

Crypto Goes Mainstream: $70 Billion ETF Assets

BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT), launched January 2024, became the fastest ETF in history to reach $10 billion in AUM — doing so in 49 days. By 2026, IBIT holds $63.5 billion+. The iShares Ethereum Trust (ETHA) adds $7.2 billion. BlackRock's endorsement of crypto ETFs fundamentally legitimised institutional crypto investment worldwide.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is BlackRock's net worth or market cap in 2026?

BlackRock has a market cap of approximately $173 billion (~₹14.4 lakh crore) as of early 2026, managing $11.5 trillion in AUM.

Is BlackRock the same as Blackstone?

No. BlackRock originated as a unit of Blackstone in 1988 but became fully independent in 1994. They are two completely separate, publicly listed companies.

What is BlackRock's crypto exposure?

BlackRock manages the iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT) with $63.5B+ AUM and the iShares Ethereum Trust (ETHA) with $7.2B+ AUM — total crypto ETF AUM exceeds $70 billion.
