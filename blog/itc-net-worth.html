The ITC Business Empire

Four Major Segments

  • Cigarettes: ITC is India's dominant tobacco company (Gold Flake, Classic, Navy Cut). ~42% of revenue, ~80% of profits. Funds everything else.
  • FMCG: Aashirvaad, Sunfeast, Yippee, Bingo, Classmate, Wills Lifestyle, Fiama, Engage. Fastest-growing segment. Yoga Bar (Sproutlife) acquired 47.5%+ April 2026.
  • Agribusiness: ITC eChoupal — India's largest e-commerce network for farmers; procures tobacco, wheat, soya, spices
  • Hotels (demerged): ITC Hotels — Welcomed, Fortune, WelcomHeritage brands; now a separately listed company

The Hotels Demerger Explained

ITC demerged its hotels business with effect from January 1, 2025. ITC Hotels Limited listed on BSE and NSE on January 29, 2025. Shareholders received 1 ITC Hotels share for every 10 ITC shares held. ITC retained a 40% strategic stake in ITC Hotels. The demerger allows ITC Hotels to access capital independently and pursue hospitality-focused growth without cigarette segment optics affecting valuations.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is ITC's net worth or market cap in 2026?

ITC Limited has a market capitalisation of approximately ₹3,94,553 crore (~$47 billion) as of April 30, 2026. Share price is approximately ₹314.90.

Who owns ITC?

ITC has no promoter family. BAT (British American Tobacco) is the largest single shareholder at approximately 25%. LIC, mutual funds, FIIs and retail investors collectively own the rest.

What happened with the ITC Hotels demerger?

ITC Hotels was demerged effective January 1, 2025, and listed on January 29, 2025. ITC retained 40% stake. Shareholders received 1 ITC Hotels share for every 10 ITC shares.
