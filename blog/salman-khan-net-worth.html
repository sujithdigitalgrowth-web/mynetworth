How Salman Khan Built His Fortune

Salman Khan's commercial debut in 1989 with Maine Pyar Kiya was a defining family-drama blockbuster. Hum Aapke Hain Koun in 1994 broke records of the era. The 2010 release of Dabangg recalibrated his persona around the masala-action genre. The Tiger franchise, the Dabangg series, and Bajrangi Bhaijaan (2015) made him Bollywood's most bankable opening-weekend star for over a decade.

The wealth inflexion was Bigg Boss. Khan has hosted continuously since Season 4 in 2010, and the per-season fee has compounded from a few crores then to a reported ₹250 crore for Season 18 (2024–25). The format is structurally tied to his name, with Colors paying both for his draw and for the security requirements his situation now demands.

Income Sources in May 2026

Per-film fees of ₹100 crore-plus are often structured with a profit-share component. After four consecutive flops (Antim, Kisi Ka Bhai Kisi Ki Jaan, Tiger 3, Sikandar), the profit share has not paid out recently, but the upfront fee remains intact. Bigg Boss generated ₹250 crore for Season 18. Season 19 reportedly ran at ₹120–150 crore for the first three months before switching to guest hosts. Brand endorsements at ₹7–10 crore per deal across 15–20 active partnerships total ₹150–300 crore annually.

Real Estate and Property Holdings

Galaxy Apartments in Bandra is the primary residence, now fortified with bulletproof glass and electric fencing after the April 2024 shooting incident and subsequent Lawrence Bishnoi gang threats. Mumbai Police implemented enhanced security protocols in May 2025. Arpita Farms in Panvel is a 150-acre farmhouse valued at approximately ₹80 crore, serving as Salman's fitness sanctuary, horse-riding base, and celebrity gathering venue. Additional holdings include a Dubai apartment at The Address Downtown and a Gorai beach home in Mumbai.

Cars and Notable Assets

Khan's car collection includes a Mercedes-Benz GLS, Range Rover Vogue, Audi RS7, BMW X6, and Lexus LX 470, plus a collection of custom-built motorbikes including the Suzuki Hayabusa. The total auto collection value is estimated at ₹15–20 crore — modest by A-list Bollywood standards and consistent with a low-profile approach to personal assets outside the Bandra residence.

Business Stakes and Investments in 2026

Salman Khan Films (SKF) is the production banner behind Tiger 3, Sikandar, and Kisi Ka Bhai Kisi Ki Jaan. Salman Khan Ventures (SKV) is the diversified holding entity covering apparel, jewellery, and several investment positions. Being Human Clothing routes profits to the Being Human Foundation (education and healthcare). He also holds a small stake in Yatra Online and select Indian startup positions.
