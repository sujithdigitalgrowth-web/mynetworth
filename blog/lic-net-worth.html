From 245 Insurers to One National Institution

LIC's History

  • September 1, 1956: Parliament passes the Life Insurance Corporation Act
  • 245 private insurers + 75 provident fund societies merged into one public entity
  • Starting capital: ₹5 crore from Government of India
  • May 2022: India's largest-ever IPO at ₹949/share (raised ~₹21,000 crore, 3.5% stake)
  • 2025: Rated 3rd strongest insurance brand globally (Brand Finance Global 500)
  • April 13, 2026: Board approves 1:1 bonus share issue — doubling total shares

What is LIC's ₹57 Lakh Crore AUM?

LIC's ₹57.05 lakh crore (~$680 billion) AUM makes it the largest domestic institutional investor in India — larger than all domestic mutual funds combined. This money is deployed in government securities, corporate bonds, equity (LIC is a top-5 shareholder in most Nifty 50 companies), and real estate. LIC's equity portfolio alone is estimated at ₹12–14 lakh crore.
