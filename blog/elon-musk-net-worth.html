How Elon Musk Built His Fortune

Musk's wealth runs on five engines: Tesla, SpaceX, xAI (now folded into SpaceX), X (formerly Twitter), and Neuralink. The first cheque came from Zip2, which he sold to Compaq for $307 million in 1999. PayPal followed in 2002 with a sale to eBay that put roughly $165 million in his pocket. Both exits were the seed capital for everything that came next.

SpaceX was incorporated in 2002 with a goal that sounded delusional at the time — reusable orbital rockets and Mars settlement. Two decades later, SpaceX is the dominant launch provider on the planet and the parent entity for Starlink, the satellite-internet network with over 7 million subscribers. Tesla came later, with Musk joining as chairman in 2004 and stepping into the CEO role in 2008. The 2018 compensation package, restored by the Delaware Supreme Court in December 2025, granted him 303.9 million Tesla shares tied to operational milestones. Most of those milestones were hit, which is why a single Tesla rally moves his net worth by tens of billions in a session.

Income Sources in May 2026

Musk takes no traditional Tesla salary. His compensation is built around equity grants, the largest of which is the November 2025 shareholder-approved $1 trillion Tesla pay package, contingent on Tesla hitting a $4.5 trillion market capitalisation, 20 million annual deliveries, 1 million Optimus robots, and 1 million robotaxis.

SpaceX equity contributes the largest paper-wealth bucket today. Following the February 2026 all-stock merger that absorbed xAI into SpaceX at a combined $1.25 trillion valuation, his SpaceX-xAI stake alone is worth more than $500 billion. Smaller positions matter too. Neuralink raised at an $8.8 billion valuation in June 2025 with Musk as the largest shareholder. The Boring Company was last marked at $7.7 billion in 2022. X, now a SpaceX subsidiary, carries a $15 to $20 billion implied valuation. None of these generate cash dividends, which is why Musk frequently borrows against his Tesla shares rather than selling them, even at this scale.

Real Estate and Property Holdings

Musk publicly stated in 2020 that he had sold most personal homes. The reality on paper looks different. In Austin alone, contiguous acquisitions through controlled entities total around $2.4 billion, including a 14,400 square foot Tuscan mansion and an adjoining six-bedroom property. Outside the city, in Bastrop County, Texas, sits Snailbrook, a Musk-affiliated town development.

Texas property records reviewed by CRE Daily and The Real Deal in 2025 show Musk-controlled entities holding 500-plus properties across roughly 6,000 acres, valued at approximately $2.4 billion. Most of this is operational land for SpaceX Starbase, the Boring Company, Tesla Gigafactory, and the new xAI Terafab chip initiative at Austin's Seaholm Power Plant. It is real estate as industrial infrastructure rather than as a lifestyle portfolio.

Cars, Jets, and Notable Assets

The personal aviation fleet is where the consumer-style spend shows up. The Falcon Landing LLC fleet, valued north of $100 million, includes a Gulfstream G700 (delivered in 2025, the flagship at roughly $70–80 million), a Gulfstream G650ER for primary long-haul, a Gulfstream G550, and a Dassault Falcon 900B. JetSpy flight-tracking data showed roughly 1,161 flight hours across 441 flights in 2023 — an average of more than three hours in the air per day.

Business Stakes and Investments in 2026

The cleanest way to read Musk's net worth is as a stake table. Tesla equity is roughly 15 percent direct, climbing to 22–28 percent diluted after the restored 2018 award. Combined SpaceX-xAI runs at 43 percent economic equity and 79 percent voting control. He is the largest individual shareholder in Neuralink, the majority owner of The Boring Company, and the controlling shareholder of X via the SpaceX-xAI structure.

The $300 million in inter-company transactions disclosed in Tesla's May 2026 10-K filing — covering money flowing between Tesla, SpaceX, xAI, and The Boring Company — illustrates how interlinked the empire is. A single SpaceX IPO at the rumoured $2.75 trillion valuation would push Musk's personal net worth past the $1 trillion mark, the first individual ever to cross that threshold.

Where Does Elon Musk Rank Among the World's Richest?

On Bloomberg's Billionaires Index in May 2026, Musk holds the number one slot at roughly $656 billion. Larry Page is a distant second at $148 billion, followed by Mark Zuckerberg, Jeff Bezos, and Sergey Brin in the $270–280 billion range. The gap to the second-place fortune is the widest it has been since the index began tracking in 2012, and it has held above $400 billion for most of 2025 and 2026.
