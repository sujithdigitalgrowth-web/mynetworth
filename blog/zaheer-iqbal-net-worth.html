How Zaheer Iqbal Built His Profile

Zaheer Iqbal's path into Bollywood started behind the camera. He worked as an assistant director on Salman Khan's Jai Ho (2014), an industry connection enabled by his father's long association with the Khan family. Iqbal Ratansi, the patriarch, is a respected jeweller and real-estate financier whose ties with Salman Khan stretch back decades. Salman has publicly acknowledged borrowing money from Iqbal Ratansi during his early acting years, a debt that established the cross-generational closeness between the two families.

The on-screen debut came in 2019 with Notebook, a Salman Khan Films production opposite Pranutan Bahl. The film was a soft commercial performer but established Zaheer as a leading-man template. Double XL (2022) with Sonakshi Sinha followed three years later. The 2025–2026 release calendar lists no major theatrical lead role yet, leaving him with a relatively thin filmography for an actor approaching his late thirties.

Income Sources in May 2026

  • Acting fees: modest given the limited filmography. Industry estimates place per-film fees at ₹50 lakh to ₹2 crore for supporting and lead roles.
  • Brand endorsements: fashion, lifestyle, and watch categories form the core, though brand-deal volumes are smaller than for higher-profile actors.
  • Family business inflows: the largest indirect contribution. Iqbal Ratansi's portfolio includes Stelmac Developers (2005–2011), Blackstone Housing & Infrastructure, and Films Tools Lights And Grip LLP (2016, Bollywood lighting equipment), plus the family jewellery house. Family-trust dividends and asset transfers form the substantive part of Zaheer's personal balance sheet.

Real Estate and Property Holdings

The couple's primary residence is a sea-facing apartment in 81 Aureate, Bandra West, valued at approximately ₹14 crore per Auto Guide Point. Sonakshi has reportedly listed her separate Bandra residence (the venue of their June 2024 wedding) for ₹25 crore. The Ratansi family also holds property across Mumbai through the various development entities listed under Iqbal Ratansi's name, though specific addresses are not publicly disclosed.

Cars and Notable Assets

The reported garage includes a Mercedes-Benz M-Class, a BMW i7, and a Ducati Scrambler. The collection is modest by Bollywood A-list standards but solid for an actor at his career stage. The choice mix, particularly the Ducati, reflects a personal preference rather than a status-display approach to car ownership.

Business Stakes and Investments in 2026

No publicly disclosed direct directorships or significant business stakes appear in Zaheer Iqbal's name as of May 2026. The family wealth flows through Iqbal Ratansi's three Stelmac/Blackstone/Films Tools entities, plus the jewellery house. Zaheer's sister Sanam Ratansi runs an independent celebrity styling practice. The family-business architecture suggests his future personal wealth growth will come more from inheritance and family-trust pathways than from individual entrepreneurship, unless his acting trajectory accelerates.
