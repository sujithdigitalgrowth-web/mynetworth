How Srikanth Bolla Built His Fortune

Born blind to a farming family in Seetharamapuram, Andhra Pradesh, Bolla fought a legal battle in his teens to study science despite Indian regulations that pushed visually impaired students towards arts streams. He won. MIT then accepted him as the first international visually impaired student in its history. He returned to India after graduating and founded Bollant Industries in 2012, producing eco-friendly disposables with a deliberate hiring practice placing persons with disabilities in roughly half the workforce.

Early investors Ratan Tata, Satya Nadella, and Srini Raju lent credibility that smoothed subsequent fundraises. The 2024 biopic Srikanth, starring Rajkummar Rao, drove significant brand visibility. Rajkummar Rao won nine-plus awards in six months for the role, with National Award speculation in 2025. The biopic effect on speaking fees and investor interest was substantial.

Bollant Industries: The Engine of the Wealth

Founded in 2012 in Hyderabad. Six manufacturing units across Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and Maharashtra. Annual turnover crossed $1 million in 2025. Headcount above 500, with a large share differently abled. Products include areca leaf plates, paper cups, biodegradable food packaging, and industrial pulp packaging. The November 2024 Bengaluru Innovation Lab focuses on smart biodegradable polymers. The January 2025 announcement of ₹300 crore international expansion into Vietnam and Indonesia, with facilities targeted by Q4 2025, positions Bollant as one of India's leading clean-tech manufacturing exporters.
