Axis Bank Net Worth in 2026: ₹4 Lakh Crore Private Bank Without a Promoter Family

Updated May 2026 · 8 min read

Axis Bank is India's third-largest private bank by market cap — and the most unusual. Its promoter holding is just 8.14%. There's no founder family, no business house. The bank is owned almost entirely by institutions, making it a one-of-a-kind governance story in Indian banking.

Axis Bank trades on the BSE and NSE as AXISBANK with a market cap of approximately ₹4 lakh crore. It was originally listed November 16, 1998 as UTI Bank before rebranding in 2007.

The Unusual Shareholding: 8.14% Promoter Holding

Who Owns Axis Bank?

  • SUUTI (Specified Undertaking of UTI) — the largest single promoter entity
  • LIC — Life Insurance Corporation of India
  • GIC — General Insurance Corporation
  • Public-sector insurers — various government insurance entities
  • Total promoter: just 8.14% — the remaining 91.86% is institutional and public

Axis Bank was set up in 1993 as UTI Bank — promoted by institutional shareholders rather than a founder family. Over three decades, SUUTI gradually sold down its stake to fund government balance-sheet needs. This leaves no controlling family, making Axis a professionally managed institution with a different accountability profile than ICICI, HDFC or Kotak.

FY26 Financial Performance

FY26 was a year of steady execution for Axis. Total revenue grew to ₹1,32,538 crore. Net profit reached ₹26,548 crore. Q4 FY26 net profit was ₹7,602.63 crore (up 1.71% YoY, 8.44% QoQ). The bank operates 4,000+ branches and 13,000+ ATMs, plus international offices in Singapore, Hong Kong, Dubai and Colombo.

The Burgundy Private and Citibank Edge

Axis's Burgundy Private wealth platform is a meaningful fee-income contributor. The cards portfolio — boosted by the Citibank India consumer business acquisition completed in 2023 — is a high-margin engine. The bank also committed ₹1,500 crore into subsidiary Axis Finance in FY26.
