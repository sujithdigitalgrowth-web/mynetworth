The ₹20 Flat Fee That Disrupted Indian Broking

The Revenue Model

  • Intraday / F&O: ₹20 per executed order — flat, regardless of trade size
  • Equity delivery: Free — zero brokerage, zero commission
  • Float income: Earnings on client fund balances parked in liquid instruments
  • Coin: Direct mutual fund platform — no distributor commission; Zerodha earns on transactions
  • Zerodha AMC: Asset management arm, launched to capture long-term SIP investors
  • Name origin: "Zero" + Sanskrit "rodha" (barrier) = removing barriers to investing

Nikhil Kamath (born September 5, 1986, Shimoga, Karnataka) dropped out after 10th standard. Chess prodigy who started trading at 17. Co-founded True Beacon (institutional wealth management) in 2020 and Gruhas (real estate investment fund) in 2021. In June 2023, he signed The Giving Pledge — committing the majority of his wealth to philanthropic causes — making him the youngest Indian to do so.

Nithin Kamath was a sub-broker from 2006, trading his own and clients' money. Recognising that technology could democratise investing, he built Kite — Zerodha's trading platform — which became India's most-used retail brokerage interface.
