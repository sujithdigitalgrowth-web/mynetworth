98 Years of Multi-Generational Wealth Building

Four Generations, One Conglomerate

  • Jamnalal Bajaj (1926): Founded in Mumbai as a trading company; close associate of Gandhi
  • Kamalnayan Bajaj: Expanded into scooters, three-wheelers, cement; Cambridge educated
  • Ramkrishna Bajaj: Led the iconic 1972 Bajaj Chetak launch
  • Rahul Bajaj: Built global presence; passed away 2022 at age 83
  • Current generation: Sanjiv (Bajaj Finserv), Rajiv (Bajaj Auto), Niraj (Bajaj Auto Chairman)

The Key Businesses

Bajaj Finance — one of India's largest NBFCs with a market cap of ₹5.83 lakh crore, 26 product lines, 51 product variants. The iconic EMI card and Flexi loan serve retail, MSME and commercial consumers.

Bajaj Finserv — the financial services holding company under Sanjiv Bajaj, housing Bajaj Allianz Life Insurance, Bajaj Allianz General Insurance, Bajaj Finance and the 2023-launched Bajaj Finserv Mutual Fund.

Bajaj Auto — the world's 4th largest two- and three-wheeler manufacturer. Pulsar motorcycles, Chetak scooter, Bajaj three-wheelers (auto-rickshaws), KTM and Triumph motorcycle partnerships.

The 2007 Demerger That Unlocked Billions

The 2007 demerger of Bajaj Auto into three separate listed entities (Bajaj Auto, Bajaj Finserv, and Bajaj Holdings & Investment) was one of the most significant corporate restructurings in Indian business history. Separating the auto business from financial services from the investment holding company allowed each to scale independently — and it unleashed Bajaj Finance into one of the most successful NBFCs of the past decade.
