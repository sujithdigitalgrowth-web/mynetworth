How Nikhil Kamath Built His Fortune

Nikhil Kamath dropped out in grade 9 and began trading equities at age 17 from a small setup in Bengaluru. By 19 he was running proprietary trading operations. In 2010, he co-founded Zerodha with brother Nithin Kamath, building India's first flat-fee discount brokerage. The model disrupted the Indian broking industry: instead of percentage-based commission, Zerodha charged a flat ₹20 per order. The business was bootstrapped — no VC funding, ever — and grew to become India's largest retail broker by active clients, handling over 10 million active traders by 2025.

Income Sources in May 2026

Zerodha profit share is the dominant income source. Zerodha's revenue was ₹4,700+ crore in FY24 with profits above ₹2,800 crore; the Kamath brothers jointly hold 60%+ and no institutional shareholders take a cut. True Beacon AIF (founded 2020) charges ultra-HNI management and performance fees. Gruhas generates property investment returns and fund-management fees. Podcast monetisation and brand partnerships on the WTF platform. Rainmatter Capital deploys capital and earns returns on 47 startup positions.

Real Estate and Notable Assets

Nikhil Kamath's personal real estate is deliberately low-profile, consistent with a publicly stated minimalist approach. He is understood to own residential property in Bengaluru, the city where Zerodha is based, but specific valuations are not in the public record. The Gruhas entity's real estate investments run into hundreds of crores but are held in the fund structure, not personally.

Where Does Nikhil Kamath Rank Among India's Wealthiest People?

At ₹27,500 crore ($3.3 billion), Nikhil Kamath ranks comfortably in India's top-100 individual wealth holders. He is the youngest person in the club and the first billionaire to have built their fortune primarily through a zero-institutional-funding bootstrapped fintech. His brother Nithin Kamath (joint Zerodha co-promoter) holds a comparable position; together the Kamath family controls one of the most unusual billionaire formations in global tech: a bootstrapped profit machine with no VC exit event.
