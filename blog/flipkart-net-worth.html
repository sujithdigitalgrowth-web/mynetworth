From ₹4 Lakh to $16 Billion: The Flipkart Story

Key Milestones

  • October 2007: Sachin Bansal + Binny Bansal launch Flipkart in Koramangala 2BHK; first book sold — "Leaving Microsoft to Change the World"
  • 2010: Cash on delivery (COD) introduced — unlocked mass-market India
  • 2014: Myntra acquired; $1B single-day Big Billion Days sale
  • May 2018: Walmart acquires 77% for $16 billion — India's largest M&A deal
  • December 2022: PhonePe demerged at $12 billion valuation
  • December 2024: NCLT approves reverse flip from Singapore back to India
  • 2026–27: IPO confirmed by Walmart CFO

Where Are the Founders Now?

Sachin Bansal (~$1–1.2B net worth) left Flipkart after the Walmart deal and founded Navi Technologies in 2018 — a diversified financial services startup (insurance, loans, mutual funds). Navi's IPO was withdrawn in 2022 but is expected to refile.

Binny Bansal (~$1.4B net worth) exited Flipkart in 2023 and has been quietly investing in early-stage startups through his investment vehicle xto10x Technologies. Kalyan Krishnamurthy remains CEO of Flipkart with an estimated ~$150M in vested equity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Flipkart's net worth or valuation in 2026?

Flipkart's current estimated valuation is $36–40 billion. It is targeting a $60–70 billion IPO valuation in 2026–27, with an IPO size target of $8–10 billion.

Who owns Flipkart?

Walmart owns approximately 85% of Flipkart after its $16 billion acquisition of 77% in May 2018.

Who founded Flipkart?

Flipkart was co-founded in October 2007 by Sachin Bansal and Binny Bansal (no family relation), both IIT Delhi graduates and former Amazon engineers.
