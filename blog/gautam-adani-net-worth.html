How Gautam Adani Built His Fortune

Adani left school in Class 10 and moved to Mumbai at age 16 in 1978, working as a diamond sorter in Mahipalpur. He returned to Ahmedabad to join his brother's plastics business, then pivoted to commodities trading — founding Adani Exports in 1988. The transformation into an infrastructure empire began with the Mundra port development in 1995 (APSEZ became India's largest port operator). Adani Power IPO (2009), Adani Wilmar JV (1999 with Wilmar International), and Adani Green (2015) followed sequentially. The aggressive 2019-22 phase added Mumbai Airport, the Holcim cement acquisition (Ambuja + ACC, 2022 for ~$10.5 billion), and NDTV (2022). Each port handles roughly 30% of India's containerised cargo.

The Hindenburg Crash and the Recovery

In January 2023, Hindenburg Research published a short-seller report alleging financial fraud across Adani Group. Market cap of the 10 Adani entities lost nearly $120 billion in 10 days. By Q2 2024, share prices had recovered. The April 2026 milestone — reclaiming Asia's richest — marks the most complete institutional recovery from a short-seller attack in modern Indian market history. Adani Green's rally was the primary driver.
