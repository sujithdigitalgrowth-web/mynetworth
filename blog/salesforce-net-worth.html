Salesforce Net Worth in 2026: $170 Billion CRM and AI Empire

Updated May 2026 · 8 min read

Salesforce, the company that pioneered the cloud software business model in 1999, reached a strange inflection point in 2026. By the numbers, FY26 was a record year: revenue of USD 41.5 billion, free cash flow of USD 14.4 billion, USD 14.3 billion returned to shareholders, and a USD 50 billion share repurchase authorisation. Yet the stock fell roughly 30 per cent during the year, with market capitalisation settling around USD 170 billion as of late April 2026. The Salesforce net worth in 2026 captures both achievements: a profoundly profitable franchise and a market that has decided to discount it.

The narrative tension is what investors are calling the &quot;SaaSpocalypse,&quot; the worry that AI-native software could disrupt traditional SaaS subscriptions. Founder and CEO Marc Benioff has been characteristically pugnacious in pushing back, arguing that Salesforce has rebuilt itself as the operating system for the &quot;Agentic Enterprise.&quot; Whether the next twelve months prove him right will depend largely on Agentforce, the company's AI-agent platform, which finished FY26 with USD 800 million in annual recurring revenue, up 169 per cent year on year.

Salesforce Net Worth at a Glance

Salesforce trades on NYSE under the ticker CRM with a market capitalisation of approximately USD 170 billion as of late April 2026. The stock closed at USD 180.18 on 27 April 2026, down 31.9 per cent year to date from a December 2025 close of USD 264.91. The 52-week range runs from a low around USD 184 to a high above USD 360, capturing the broad de-rating that has hit growth software through 2026.

FY26, which ended 31 January 2026, delivered revenue of USD 41.5 billion (up 10 per cent year on year), Q4 revenue of USD 11.2 billion (up 12 per cent), and total remaining performance obligation of USD 72.4 billion (up 14 per cent), the contracted future revenue waiting to be recognised. Operating cash flow ran at USD 15 billion and free cash flow at USD 14.4 billion. The company guided FY27 revenue to USD 45.8 to 46.2 billion, representing 10 to 11 per cent growth.

Marc Benioff: Founder, Chairman, and CEO

Marc Benioff founded Salesforce in 1999 in a one-bedroom apartment near his San Francisco home, with the now-iconic &quot;No Software&quot; logo as his statement of intent. He had spent his career at Oracle, where he became the youngest vice-president in the firm's history at age 26. His thesis was that business software should be delivered through a web browser, paid for as a subscription, and updated continuously, an idea that was novel enough at the time to be ridiculed by many of the industry's incumbents.

Twenty-seven years later, Benioff still holds both the Chairman and CEO roles, an unusual concentration of authority for a public company of this scale. His personal stake in Salesforce, combined with secondary investments in Time magazine (which he and his wife acquired in 2018) and other ventures, places his net worth at approximately USD 11 billion as of early 2026 according to Forbes Real-Time Billionaires. He has been an active philanthropist through the 1-1-1 model (one per cent of equity, time and product to charitable causes), which Salesforce pioneered and many other tech firms have since adopted.

Agentforce: The AI Bet

Agentforce is Salesforce's strategic answer to the question of whether AI replaces SaaS or extends it. Launched in late 2024, the platform allows businesses to deploy AI agents that take actions inside Salesforce's existing customer relationship management workflows. The numbers from Q4 FY26 were striking: USD 800 million in annual recurring revenue (up 169 per cent year on year), 29,000 deals closed (up 50 per cent quarter on quarter), and 2.4 billion &quot;agentic work units&quot; delivered to date.

Agentforce 1 Edition and Agentforce for Apps, the most premium SKUs, saw new bookings nearly triple quarter on quarter. More than 60 per cent of Agentforce and Data 360 bookings came from existing customers expanding their commitments rather than from new logos, suggesting the product is succeeding as an upsell motion rather than as a standalone customer acquisition tool. The company has set a long-term target of USD 63 billion in revenue by FY30, implying an 11 per cent compound annual growth rate from FY26 levels.

Informatica Acquisition and the Data Strategy

In Q1 FY27 (which started 1 February 2026), Salesforce closed its acquisition of Informatica, the data management specialist, for approximately USD 8 billion. Informatica contributed USD 399 million to Q4 FY26 revenue ahead of the formal close. Management has guided that the acquisition adds approximately 3 percentage points to FY27 revenue growth and is expected to be accretive within two years post-close, reflecting the speed of integration the company is targeting.

The strategic logic is straightforward: AI agents are only as good as the data they have access to, and Informatica's data harmonisation, governance and quality tools sit upstream of every meaningful Agentforce deployment. Salesforce's earlier acquisitions (Tableau in 2019 for USD 15.7 billion, Slack in 2021 for USD 27.7 billion, MuleSoft in 2018 for USD 6.5 billion) have followed similar logic of adding adjacent capability to the core CRM platform.

Capital Returns and the Buyback Story

Salesforce returned USD 14.3 billion to shareholders in FY26 (USD 12.7 billion through buybacks and USD 1.6 billion through dividends), representing 99 per cent of free cash flow. The board approved a 5.8 per cent increase in the quarterly dividend to USD 0.44 per share and authorised a fresh USD 50 billion share repurchase programme, replacing all prior authorisations. Benioff publicly framed the increased buyback intensity as taking advantage of &quot;low prices,&quot; given the year-to-date stock decline.

This is a notable shift in posture for a company that historically reinvested most cash flow into product development and acquisitions. The combination of a maturing growth profile and a depressed share price has tilted the capital allocation philosophy toward returning more cash to shareholders. Some analysts have begun modelling a leveraged buyout scenario, given the predictable subscription cash flows and depressed valuation, though Benioff's continued control of the company makes this hypothetical at best.

Final Word

Salesforce's 2026 story is one of strong fundamentals trading at a discounted valuation. Revenue of USD 41.5 billion, free cash flow of USD 14.4 billion and Agentforce ARR of USD 800 million represent a healthy, scaling business. The market's question is whether AI is a tailwind or a threat, and the answer will be priced into the stock through FY27 results. Marc Benioff's bet on the Agentic Enterprise is the cleanest test of whether incumbent SaaS leaders can stay relevant as software shifts from human-driven to agent-driven.
