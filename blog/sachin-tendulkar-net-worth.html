How Sachin Tendulkar Built His Fortune

Sachin Tendulkar debuted for India at 16 years and 205 days on 15 November 1989. What followed over 24 years was the most statistically documented cricket career in history: 100 international centuries, 34,357 international runs across formats, and the 2011 World Cup as the defining team trophy. His endorsement career began in parallel with his cricket — MRF, Pepsi, Boost, Adidas — and at peak he commanded over ₹100 crore per year in brand deals alone.

The 2008 IPL auction created a new wealth layer: his ownership stake in the Kochi Tuskers (defunct) and later strategic association with Mumbai Indians. The post-retirement phase since November 2013 has been methodically built around four pillars: endorsements, MI mentorship, startup investments, and the legacy brand.

Income Sources in May 2026
