How Saif Ali Khan Built His Fortune

Saif Ali Khan is the son of cricket legend Mansoor Ali Khan Pataudi (nawab of Pataudi, Indian cricket captain) and actress Sharmila Tagore. He inherited Pataudi Palace and its associated lands in Haryana, which forms the single largest asset in his portfolio at ~₹800 crore. His acting career began with Parampara (1993) and found its commercial footing through Dil Chahta Hai (2001), Kal Ho Na Ho (2003), and Hum Tum (2004, National Award Best Actor).

From 2010 onwards, Saif diversified into production through Illuminati Films (founded 2003) — producing Love Aaj Kal (2009), Cocktail (2012), and Go Goa Gone (2013). The production company added revenue independent of acting. By 2020, his combined film fee, endorsement revenue, and Pataudi Palace income made him one of Bollywood's wealthiest actors.

Pataudi Palace and Heritage Wealth

Pataudi Palace (officially Ibrahim Kothi) in Pataudi, Haryana, is the most valuable single asset in Saif Ali Khan's portfolio. The 10-acre estate with 150+ rooms was built in 1935 for the Nawab of Pataudi. After his father Mansoor Ali Khan Pataudi's death in 2011, Saif inherited the palace along with siblings Saba and Soha Ali Khan. Its estimated value of ~₹800 crore reflects the land value, heritage building, and the Pataudi locality's appreciation as a Delhi NCR suburb.

The Bhopal Estate Dispute

The contested Bhopal assets — Flagstaff House, Noor-Us-Sabah Palace, Dar-Us-Salam, Habibi's Bungalow, Ahmedabad Palace, and Kohefiza — are collectively estimated at ₹15,000 crore. These belonged to the Nawab of Bhopal's family. Under the Enemy Property Act 1968, properties of individuals who migrated to Pakistan at Partition are vested in the Indian government. Saif's grandmother, Sajida Sultan, was the ruler of Bhopal. The Madhya Pradesh High Court reaffirmed in December 2024 that these estates are enemy property and gave the family 30 days to assert claims. The family has contested this designation consistently. As of May 2026, these assets are excluded from the net worth estimate as they are not available to Saif.

The January 2025 Knife Attack

On 16 January 2025, a domestic intruder attacked Saif Ali Khan at his 12th-floor Satguru Sharan flat in Bandra, Mumbai, inflicting 6 stab wounds. One wound was near the spine, requiring 5-hour spinal and plastic surgery at Lilavati Hospital. Saif was discharged on 21 January 2025. The attacker was arrested within days and later identified. Saif's first major project after recovery was Jewel Thief: The Heist Begins on Netflix (25 April 2025), co-starring Jaideep Ahlawat.
