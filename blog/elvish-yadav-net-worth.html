The Snake Venom Case — Supreme Court Ruling Explained

An FIR was lodged in March 2024 alleging that Elvish Yadav administered snake venom to attendees at rave parties. The Allahabad High Court refused to quash the FIR in an earlier hearing. On 19 March 2026, the Supreme Court quashed both the FIR and the chargesheet, ruling that snake-venom antibodies are not listed in the schedule of substances under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act, 1985. The ruling means no criminal liability can attach under the NDPS framework for the alleged conduct.
