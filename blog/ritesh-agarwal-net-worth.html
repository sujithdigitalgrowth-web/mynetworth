How Ritesh Agarwal Got from a Small Town to ₹16,000 Crore

Ritesh Agarwal was born on 16 November 1993 in Bissam Cuttack, a small town in Odisha, into a Marwari family. He was raised in Titilagarh, where his family ran a small shop in Rayagada. He completed school at Sacred Heart School in Rayagada and then St. John's Senior Secondary School in Kota — moving to Rajasthan as part of the well-trodden path that thousands of Indian students take to prepare for IIT entrance exams.

In 2011, at age 17 (and still preparing for IIT in Kota), Ritesh launched his first venture, Oravel Travels, an Airbnb-style platform for budget accommodation. He moved to Delhi in 2011 for college but dropped out after a few days — choosing instead to attend startup events and conferences. In 2012, he received ₹30 lakh from VentureNursery, a Mumbai-based accelerator, which became his first institutional capital.

In 2013, at age 19, he received the Thiel Fellowship — Peter Thiel's $100,000 grant for promising young founders who agreed to drop out of college. He was the first Indian to receive it. The same year, he pivoted Oravel into OYO Rooms, focusing on standardising budget hotel rooms in India. The early-stage product began with five hotels in Gurgaon. The growth from there was extraordinary — by 2018 OYO had crossed a billion-dollar valuation, and by 2019 it was operating in 80+ countries.

In 2019, Ritesh executed one of the most consequential moves in his career — the Prism Holdings transaction. Through Prism (a personal holding company), he bought back approximately $700 million worth of OYO shares from early investors including SoftBank and Lightspeed, increasing his personal stake from approximately 10% to 30%. The COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 then nearly destroyed OYO — global occupancy collapsed, the company let go of thousands of employees, and the valuation cratered from a $10–12 billion peak.

The recovery has been the defining business achievement of his career. By FY24, OYO reported its first-ever profit after tax of ₹229 crore. By FY25, profit had grown to ₹623 crore on revenue of approximately ₹6,463 crore. Q1 FY26 reported PAT of approximately ₹200 crore. PRISM filed its DRHP with SEBI in December 2025 targeting an IPO at $7–8 billion valuation.

Ritesh Agarwal: The Founder-CEO

Ritesh Agarwal married Geetansha Sood in March 2023, and the couple welcomed their son Aryan Agarwal in December 2023. His father, Ramesh Agarwal, passed away in March 2023 just days after the wedding. He owns an extensive collection of luxury vehicles including a Rolls-Royce Phantom Extended Wheelbase, Bentley Bentayga, Mercedes-Benz S-Class and Range Rover Autobiography.

Ritesh has joined Shark Tank India as a panellist starting Season 3 (which aired January–March 2024 on Sony LIV and Sony Entertainment Television). He returned for Season 4 in 2025 and Season 5 which premiered in January 2026. He is the wealthiest judge on the panel and has become one of the most active sharks — making 26 deals in Season 3 alone. Beyond OYO, he is also an active angel investor and sits on multiple advisory boards. He was named to the Forbes 30 Under 30 Asia list in 2016 and won the EY Entrepreneur of the Year in 2018.
