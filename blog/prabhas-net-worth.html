How Prabhas Built His Fortune

Prabhas (Uppalapati Venkata Suryanarayana Prabhas Raju) was born into a Telugu film family in Narsapuram, Andhra Pradesh. His uncle, the late Krishnam Raju, was a major Telugu film actor. Prabhas debuted in 2002 with Eeswar and built a steady Telugu career through films like Varsham (2004), Chatrapathi (2005), and Bujjigadu (2008). The career-defining moment came with Baahubali (2015), which he spent 5 years filming under S.S. Rajamouli. The franchise's ₹2,500+ crore gross transformed him into a pan-India and internationally known star — the first Telugu actor to achieve that scale.

Post-Baahubali, he moved into pan-India and global productions: Saaho (2019), Radhe Shyam (2022 — underperformed), Adipurush (2023 — major setback due to poor VFX reception), and Kalki 2898 AD (2024 — ₹1,000+ crore redemption). His film fees escalated with each production even through the setback period, reflecting producer confidence in his audience pull.
