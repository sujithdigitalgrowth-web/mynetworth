Anil Agarwal: From Patna Scrap Trader to ₹2.82 Lakh Crore

Anil Agarwal was born in 1954 in Patna, Bihar, to a Marwari family. He started at age 19 as a scrap-metal trader with no formal college education. He acquired Sterlite Industries (copper, aluminium, zinc), then listed Vedanta Resources on the London Stock Exchange in 2002 — the first Indian company to do so.

Key acquisitions: Hindustan Zinc (2002 privatisation), BALCO (2001), Cairn India (2011). The May 2026 demerger — his '3D strategy' (Demerger, Diversification, Deleveraging) — is the most significant restructuring of his career.

Record FY26 Performance

FY26 was Vedanta's best year ever: revenue ₹1.74 lakh crore (+15% YoY), PAT ₹25,096 crore (+22% YoY), Q4 FY26 PAT ₹9,352 crore (+89% YoY). Total shareholder return: 48.6%, outperforming the Nifty Metal Index by 2x. Full-year dividend: ₹34 per share.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Vedanta's net worth in 2026?

As of late April 2026 (pre-demerger), Vedanta Limited had a market capitalisation of approximately ₹2.82 lakh crore (~$33 billion). On May 1, 2026, it demerged into five listed entities.

What was the Vedanta demerger?

On May 1, 2026, Vedanta split into five entities: Vedanta Aluminium Metal, Vedanta Iron and Steel, Vedanta Power, Vedanta Oil and Gas, and residual Vedanta Limited holding Hindustan Zinc.

Who is the founder of Vedanta?

Vedanta was founded by Anil Agarwal, born 1954 in Patna, Bihar. He started as a scrap-metal trader at 19 and listed Vedanta Resources in London in 2002 — the first Indian company to do so.
