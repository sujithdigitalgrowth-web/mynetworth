How Virat Kohli Built His Fortune

Kohli made his India debut in ODIs in August 2008 and his Test debut in June 2011. His ascent to the number-one Test batsman ranking coincided with an endorsement inflexion that pushed his annual brand income into triple digits by 2015. He captained India across all three formats between 2014 and 2022, with the national team achieving historic Test series wins in Australia (2018–19 and 2020–21) under his leadership.

The business pivot came from 2014 onwards. One8, his lifestyle brand, launched in 2014 as a collaboration with Puma and has since expanded into restaurants, gyms, and lifestyle products. Wrogn, the fashion label targeting youth, followed. His FC Goa stake gives him a foothold in Indian club football. The April 2025 Agilitas deal — where he turned down a ₹300 crore Puma renewal to take an equity-inclusive arrangement — reflects deliberate wealth-building logic: equity over cash.

Income Sources in May 2026

Brand endorsements are the dominant income stream at ₹200 crore-plus annually. The marquee deals are MRF Tyres (₹12.5 crore per year, one of the longest-running cricket sponsorships), Audi (₹5 crore-plus per year, now equity-linked), and Puma replacement Agilitas (equity-inclusive, April 2025). Other active brands include Boost, American Tourister, Tissot, and several digital-first companies.

IPL 2026 brings ₹21 crore from RCB, where he was retained as the franchise's highest-paid player. Career IPL earnings now exceed ₹212 crore. BCCI Grade A-plus retainer contributes ₹7 crore annually (post-Test retirement restructured to Grade A). The combination of endorsement income plus IPL makes him the highest-earning Indian cricket player on a cash-income basis.
