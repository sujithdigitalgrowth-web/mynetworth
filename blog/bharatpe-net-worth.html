The Foundation: One QR Code for All UPI Apps

BharatPe was founded in 2018 by Ashneer Grover, Bhavik Koladiya and Shashvat Nakrani on a single bold insight: Indian merchants were drowning in a different QR code for every UPI app. BharatPe created one interoperable QR code that accepts all UPI payments — and charged merchants zero transaction fees while making money on merchant loans.

The Unity SFB Bet

BharatPe holds a 50% stake in Unity Small Finance Bank, giving it access to a licensed banking platform. SFBs can accept public deposits — a far more valuable (and regulated) activity than payments processing. The strategic logic: become a full-stack financial services company for Indian merchants.

Key Investors

Tiger Global, Coatue Management, Ribbit Capital, Insight Partners, Dragoneer, Sequoia Capital and Steadview Capital are among the key backers. All value was priced in at the $2.7 billion August 2021 round. There has been no public mark-up or mark-down since.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is BharatPe's valuation in 2026?

BharatPe's last priced valuation was $2.7 billion (~₹22,500 crore) from its August 2021 Series E round. There have been no new equity rounds since.

What happened to Ashneer Grover?

Ashneer Grover resigned as CEO and director of BharatPe in March 2022 following allegations of fund misappropriation. Nalin Negi was elevated as CEO in 2023 and leads the company today.

What is BharatPe's biggest strategic asset?

BharatPe holds a 50% stake in Unity Small Finance Bank (SFB), giving it access to a licensed banking platform — a rare and valuable asset in Indian fintech.
